What I learnt
The target distribution was right-skewed and artificially capped at $500,000, so I removed those rows and applied a log transform before modelling. I also learnt that raw count features such as total rooms and population carry less signal than per-household ratios, and that fitting the imputer and scaler on the training split only is what gives an honest read on generalisation.
Impact
Tuning XGBoost with 5-fold GridSearchCV over depth, learning rate, and subsample produced the best result across all models tested, reaching an R² of 0.7488 with a mean absolute error of roughly $31,400. The model and its preprocessing steps were packaged into a working Streamlit app, taking the project from notebook to a live, user-facing tool.
Skills gained
Regression modelling and evaluation, feature engineering from raw housing counts, leakage-safe preprocessing pipelines, hyperparameter tuning with GridSearchCV, model serialisation with joblib, and deployment of a trained model as a live Streamlit application.
